BITS

The first ever task-based intra-school ICT competition organized by ACICTS

What is BITS?

BITS is a task-based competition. Students have to register and claim tasks. They have to select a task, complete the task, submit the task. Then we(BITS organizing comittee) are reviewing task and accept or tell them about missings. In tasks there are three categoeries, Code, Design, Explore. All the tasks are divided into aforesaid categoery. Student can take any task from any categoery. In addition, there are some events.
  • The online test
  • ARDROBERRY
  • Programmer & Game Developer
  • Still & Motion Graphics
BITS 2018, the Intra-School practice-based ICT competition was organized by the Ananda College ICT Society for the first time in Sri Lanka with the intention of providing platform to our school students and polish their IT knowledge in the different fields of ICT. Students had to register for the competition and claim tasks. Then they had to select a task, complete the it and submit. Then we(BITS organizing comittee) the task. There were 3 tasks categoeries , Code, Design, Explore. All the tasks were divided into according to the above categoeries. Competitors were able to take any task from any categoery. In addition to the tasks, there were Events which the competitors could gain more points by completing. They were as follows. The online test ARDROBERRY Programmer & Game Developer Still & Motion Graphics We chose the best projects from the Events and the winners were able to earn more points through these. All the points from Events, weekly tests and tasks were added to the Leaderboard in order to select the winners of BITS. All the students who were doing the tasks were divided into four categoeries(Code, Design, Explore) through their progress in tasks and Events. Finally we chose three winners and two finalists who showed their talents in all three categories; Code, Design and Explore (they were called as all rounded winners). Also we chose a winner per category; Code, Design, Explore (they were called as side winners). Then we chose two winners per Event; ARDOBERRY, Programmer & Game Developer, Still & Motion Graphics as for the age category ( Grade 6-8 winnner and Grade 9-11 winner from ARDOBERRY, Grade 6-9 winnner and Grade 10-11 winner from Programmer & Game Developer, Grade 6-9 winnner and Grade 10-11 winner from Still & Motion Graphics.) These 14 students (5 all rounded winners + 3 side winners + 6 event winners) were awarded in the BITS Awarding Ceremony. Our mission is to increase the number of participants in Google Code-in (GCI) competition; the world famous competition for young students which is annualy organized by Google. So we organized a GCI workshop during the Awarding Ceremony of the BITS. We invited other leading schools in Sri Lanka to take part in this great occasion. This workshop was conducted by the Google Developer Group and other leading organizations and individuals in the ICT sector.
 
     

Events

The Online Test

A online test was done on every friday. Competitors had to answer all the questions in the given test


ADROBERRY

Competitor had  to make any embedded system for a question in the 
social using Arduino, Microbit or Raspberry.

Programmer & Game Developer

Competitor had to design any software using any language(Ex: VB, VB.net, C#, C+, C++, HTML, Java, JS, PHP, Pascal etc.) for 'Software developing' category. 

Still  & 
Motion Graphics

Competitor had to design any design related to above categories using any software

Eg : Photoshop, 3Ds Max, Blender, AfterEffects etc.
